Stars Explained

Life Cycle

The life cycle of a star is often longer than our puny human minds can comprehend. It starts in vast nebuluae, and ends either quietly, or in a blaze of glory.

All stars are made up of mostly hydrogen and helium gas. This gas is from nebulae, which are much colder than their eventual blazing counterparts.

This gas slowly clumps together due to gravity, and due to how pressure increases temperature, the nebulaic gas starts getting hotter. This process keeps going.
